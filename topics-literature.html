Literatures

Read More

Mi Último Adiós (Poem)

Translated as "My Last Farewell," this is a poem written by Rizal on the eve of his execution on December 30, 1896. It is a poignant and emotional farewell to his countrymen, urging them to continue the fight for independence

La Indolencia de los Filipinos (Essay)

Translated as "The Indolence of the Filipinos," this is an essay written by Rizal to explain the alleged "indolence" or laziness of the Filipino people as perceived by the Spanish colonizers. Rizal refuted this stereotype and provided socio-historical explanations for the conditions in the Philippines.

Sucesos de las Islas Filipinas (Annotations)

Rizal wrote annotations to Antonio de Morga's "Sucesos de las Islas Filipinas" (Events in the Philippine Islands), a historical work. Rizal's annotations aimed to correct misconceptions and provide insights into Philippine history and culture.

Sa mga Kabataan ng Malolos (Letter)

Translated as "To the Young Women of Malolos," this is a letter written by Rizal in 1889. It encourages the young women of Malolos to pursue education and advocate for their rights, challenging the oppressive Spanish colonial norms.

To the Filipino Youth (Essay)

This essay, written in 1879, emphasizes the importance of education in the development of a nation. It encourages the Filipino youth to seek knowledge and contribute to the progress of their country.

To the Flowers of Heidelberg (Poem)

This poem expresses Rizal's nostalgia for his homeland while he was studying in Heidelberg, Germany. It metaphorically addresses the flowers of Heidelberg as a reminder of his beloved Philippines.

A La Juventud Filipina (Poem)

This poem, written in 1879, is dedicated to the Filipino youth. It encourages them to rise and contribute to the welfare of the nation, emphasizing the importance of education and patriotism
