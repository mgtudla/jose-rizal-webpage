Rizal's Higher Education

Read More

Ateneo Municipal

The Ateneo Municipal de Manila, commonly referred to as Ateneo, was a Jesuit-run institution founded in 1859. Rizal enrolled at Ateneo in 1872 at the age of 11. His time at Ateneo was formative, and he excelled academically, earning top honors in his classes. He also participated in extracurricular activities such as literary and cultural societies.

Rizal's experiences at Ateneo played a significant role in shaping his intellectual development and his views on social issues. The Jesuit education he received emphasized critical thinking, moral values, and a strong sense of social responsibility, which influenced Rizal's later activism and writings advocating for social reform and Philippine independence.

Rizal graduated on March 23, 1877, attaining sobresaliente or excellent with 5 medals. He received the degree of Bachelor of Arts.

University of Sto. Thomas

1877 - 1882

While UST was under Dominicans, the rival of Jesuits that established Ateneo, Rizal remained loyal to Ateneo.

Pre-Med Course

Out of the 28 young men taking Ampliacion only four including Rizal were granted the privilege of taking simultaneously the preparatory course and the first year of medicine.

Rizal also completed his four-year practical medical training at the Hospital de San Juan de Dios in Intramuros. In his final year at the University, Rizal achieved an overall grade of Notable (Very Good) in all subjects, ranking as the second-best student in a significantly reduced class of seven who successfully completed the medicine course. Following this accomplishment, Rizal made the decision to pursue further studies in Spain.
